# Code of Conduct

## Our commitment

We — contributors, maintainers, and community members of AnvilML and the wider SindriStudio
project — are committed to making participation a harassment-free, welcoming, and productive
experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity,
sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socioeconomic
status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, respectful, and healthy
community.

## Expected behaviour

Examples of behaviour that helps create a positive environment:

- Showing empathy, patience, and kindness toward others.
- Respecting differing opinions, viewpoints, and experiences.
- Giving and gracefully accepting constructive feedback — and assuming good faith.
- Taking responsibility for our mistakes, apologising to those affected, and learning from them.
- Focusing on what is best for the community and the project, not just for ourselves.
- Keeping technical discussion technical: critique ideas and code, not people.

## Unacceptable behaviour

The following are not tolerated in any project space:

- Sexualised language or imagery, and sexual attention or advances of any kind.
- Trolling, insulting or derogatory comments, and personal or political attacks.
- Public or private harassment.
- Publishing others' private information (such as a physical or email address) without explicit
  permission ("doxxing").
- Sustained disruption of discussions, reviews, or community spaces.
- Other conduct that could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional setting.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all project spaces — the repository, issues, pull requests,
discussions, and any official chat or event — and also applies when an individual is officially
representing the project in public spaces.

## Reporting

If you experience or witness unacceptable behaviour, please report it to the project maintainers at
**`<CONDUCT_CONTACT_PLACEHOLDER>`** (e.g. `conduct@sindristudio.dev`). Reports are handled
confidentially. Include, where possible: what happened, where and when, who was involved, and any
supporting context (links, screenshots).

Maintainers are obligated to respect the privacy and safety of anyone who reports an incident.

## Enforcement

Maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing these standards and will take fair,
appropriate corrective action in response to behaviour they deem inappropriate, threatening,
offensive, or harmful. Maintainers have the right to remove, edit, or reject comments, commits,
code, issues, and other contributions that are not aligned with this Code of Conduct, and will
communicate reasons when appropriate.

Enforcement actions are applied proportionally, typically following these tiers:

1. **Correction** — A private, written reminder of the standards. A public apology may be requested.
2. **Warning** — A warning with consequences for continued behaviour, including a defined period of
   no interaction with the people involved.
3. **Temporary ban** — A temporary ban from any interaction or public communication with the
   community.
4. **Permanent ban** — A permanent ban from public interaction within the community.
